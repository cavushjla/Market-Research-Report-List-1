<p><p>Fluoro-Phosphazene Rubber is produced through different polymerization methods, each suited for specific applications. Suspension polymerization involves dispersing the reactants in a liquid medium, yielding high molecular weight rubbers with controlled particle sizes. Emulsion polymerization creates small rubber particles in an aqueous phase, allowing for rapid reactions and improved elasticity. Each method impacts the final product's characteristics, including performance, processing, and application suitability.</p></p>
