<p><ul><li>Stool Culture</li><li>ELISA</li><li>Radioimmunoassay</li><li>Tube Agglutination</li><li>CT Scan</li><li>Ultrasonography</li><li>Colonoscopy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Yersinia diagnostics market includes various testing methods to detect Yersinia infections. Stool culture remains the gold standard for identifying the bacterium in gastrointestinal samples. ELISA (enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay) provides a rapid, serological approach to detect antibodies. Radioimmunoassay employs radiolabeled antigens for sensitivity. Tube agglutination is a serological test for detecting bacteria-specific antibodies. Imaging techniques like CT scans and ultrasonography assist in diagnosing complications of Yersinia infections, while colonoscopy allows direct examination of the intestinal tract.</p></p>
