<p><p>Polyester polyols for flexible foams are produced using various melting methods that influence their properties and applications. The vacuum melting method reduces oxidation and enhances purity, resulting in superior foam quality. The carrier gas melting method utilizes an inert gas to facilitate processing, improving thermal stability and consistency. The azeotropic vapor method employs solvent mixtures to lower boiling points, allowing for efficient extraction and production of high-performance polyols. Each method impacts the foam's performance characteristics and market preferences.</p></p>
